Ministry of Communications

Maintaining the transparency, integrity, and reach of the national narrative — ensuring information flows efficiently within the nation and that our global digital presence accurately reflects our values and progress.

Active
What We Do

Responsibilities

The core duties of this ministry

Official Statements
Composes and releases all formal statements made in the name of the Republic — from policy positions to national addresses. Every word published here is deliberate and authorised.
In-World Broadcasts
Manages the physical communication layer inside Nomashae's territory — notice boards, announcement signs, and in-world bulletins posted across cities and regions for citizens to read directly.
Narrative Integrity
Monitors how Nomashae is represented and discussed. When the national story is misrepresented — inside or outside the Republic — this ministry identifies it and responds through official channels.
Inter-Ministry Coordination
Acts as the communications bridge between ministries. When multiple departments need to speak with one voice on a shared issue, this ministry ensures their messaging is consistent and clear.
Press Releases & Bulletins
Produces formal press releases for major government decisions. Written to inform, not to spin — released across official platforms and kept in the public record indefinitely.
Public Awareness
Ensures citizens are not only informed after decisions are made, but kept aware of what is being worked on and discussed. Transparency is not just a standard here — it is the default.
How We Operate

Press & Communication Standards

Every message published by this ministry is held to these standards — no exceptions

Verified Before Published
No statement is released without being reviewed for accuracy. If a fact cannot be confirmed, it is not included. If something later turns out to be wrong, a public correction is issued — not a quiet edit.
Neutral in Tone, Honest in Content
Official communications do not editorialize. The government's words represent all of Nomashae — not a political lean or personal view. Facts are presented as facts, decisions as decisions.
Written for Citizens, Not Officials
Language is clear and accessible. Government communications are not written to impress — they are written so that every citizen, regardless of their familiarity with policy, can understand what is being said.
Timely — But Never Rushed Into Error
Speed matters, but a fast wrong statement causes more damage than a careful correct one. The standard is: as quickly as accuracy allows — and not a moment sooner.
Corrections Are Made in Public
When an error appears in an official statement, it is corrected through a follow-up release on the same channels it was originally published. Citizens deserve to know when something was wrong.
Where We Speak

Official Broadcast Platforms

The three channels through which Nomashae communicates with its citizens

Primary
Official Website
The authoritative record of all national communications. Press releases, ministry updates, and formal statements are published here first and stay here permanently. If it is not on the website, it is not official.

In-World
