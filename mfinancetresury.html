What We Do

Responsibilities

The pillars of Nomashae's financial governance

National Budget
Planning, allocating, and overseeing the Republic's annual budget. Every ministry receives funding through the Treasury — accountably, transparently, and fairly.
Currency & Monetary Policy
Managing the Nomashalian Pice — the Republic's official currency. The Ministry sets monetary policy to maintain stability and ensure the Pice retains its value.
Economic Development
Growing Nomashae's economy through smart policy, investment in public works, and support for citizen entrepreneurship. A strong economy means a strong Republic.
Taxation & Revenue
Designing and administering Nomashae's taxation system — fair, simple, and proportional. Revenue collected funds public services, infrastructure, and national programmes.
Trade & Economic Relations
Overseeing Nomashae's trade relationships with outside parties. The Ministry negotiates terms that benefit the Republic's citizens while upholding its values.
Treasury Management
Safeguarding the Republic's financial reserves and public assets. The Treasury maintains Nomashae's solvency and ensures funds are available when needed.
Official Currency

The Nomashalian Pice

Nomashae's own currency — issued and managed by the Ministry of Finance & Treasury, minted in three tiers

⬤
Copper Pice
Low Denomination · Everyday Use

The most common Pice in circulation. Copper Pice are used for everyday transactions and small purchases across all regions of Nomashae. Accessible to every citizen.

1 Copper 5 Copper 10 Copper
⬤
Silver Pice
Mid Denomination · Trade & Services

The standard currency for trade, services, and ministry transactions. Silver Pice bridge the gap between everyday spending and major purchases — the backbone of Nomashae's economy.

1 Silver 5 Silver 10 Silver
⬤
Gold Pice
High Denomination · National Reserve

The highest denomination of the Nomashalian Pice. Gold Pice are used for large government transactions, national infrastructure funding, and as the Republic's reserve currency. Rare and valuable.

1 Gold 5 Gold 10 Gold

Exchange Rates

10 Copper Pice = 1 Silver Pice  ·  10 Silver Pice = 1 Gold Pice
All exchange rates are set and maintained by the Ministry of Finance & Treasury. Rates are reviewed periodically to reflect the economic conditions of the Republic.
