What We Do

Responsibilities

How the Ministry represents and protects Nomashae on the world stage

Diplomatic Relations
Establishing, maintaining, and developing Nomashae's relationships with other nations, micronations, communities, and organisations. Every relationship begins with respect.
Treaties & Agreements
Negotiating and ratifying formal agreements with outside parties — from trade deals and mutual recognition treaties to cooperation accords and peace agreements.
International Representation
Speaking on behalf of Nomashae in external forums, communities, and events. The Ministry ensures the Republic's voice is heard clearly, consistently, and with dignity.
External Intelligence
Monitoring geopolitical developments relevant to Nomashae's interests. Working alongside the NIaI to keep the government informed of external threats, opportunities, and shifts in the wider world.
Citizen Representation Abroad
Supporting Nomashalian citizens operating or engaging outside the Republic's borders. Citizens travelling or conducting affairs externally may contact the Ministry for guidance.
Peace & Mediation
Where conflicts affect Nomashae or its allies, the Ministry leads mediation efforts. In the tradition of Nomashae's founding principles — diplomacy before all else.
Our Position

Diplomatic Stance

What Nomashae will and will not do in its external relations

Nomashae Will
Engage any nation or community in good-faith dialogue.
Pursue mutual recognition with other micronations.
Honour all treaties and agreements it signs in full.
Offer mediation where conflicts arise near its interests.
Represent its citizens' interests abroad with full commitment.
Share its culture, language, and values openly with the world.
Nomashae Will Not
Enter any alliance that compromises its sovereignty or values.
Engage in hostile diplomacy or political aggression.
Sign agreements that conflict with its Constitution.
Recognise nations whose values contradict Nomashae's core principles.
Allow external pressure to override internal democratic decisions.
Take sides in external conflicts that do not threaten the Republic.
The Process

How Nomashae Forms Relations

The steps from first contact to a formal relationship with another nation or party

1
First Contact An outside party or Nomashae itself initiates contact. The Ministry of External Affairs receives and reviews the outreach, assessing the nature and intent of the relationship.
2
Diplomatic Review The Ministry reviews the outside party — their values, governance, and interests — to determine whether a relationship is in Nomashae's best interest.
3
Dialogue & Negotiation If approved, both parties open dialogue. Terms of any agreement, recognition, or cooperation are negotiated openly and in good faith.
4
Parliamentary Approval Any formal treaty or agreement must be approved by Parliament before it is ratified. No binding external commitment is made without the consent of Nomashae's citizens.
5
Presidential Ratification The President signs and ratifies the agreement on behalf of the Republic. The treaty is then documented in the national archive and takes effect immediately.

Citizen Services

What citizens can contact this ministry for

Request Diplomatic Contact
Know of a community, micronation, or party that Nomashae should reach out to? Citizens can submit diplomatic contact requests to the Ministry for review.
External Representation Support
Citizens representing Nomashae in external spaces — online or otherwise — can request official backing or credentials from the Ministry.

If a citizen encounters a situation involving Nomashae's external interests — hostile contact, impersonation, or diplomatic incidents — report it to the Ministry immediately.
