What We Do

Responsibilities

The pillars of Nomashae's physical foundation

Roads & Pathways
Planning, constructing, and maintaining all roads, bridges, and transit routes across Nomashae's regions. Connecting every corner of the Republic.
Public Buildings
Overseeing the design, construction, and upkeep of all government buildings, civic structures, and public facilities throughout Nomashae.
Utilities & Services
Managing the Republic's essential utilities — power, water supply, and sanitation — ensuring every region has access to reliable public services.
Public Spaces
Developing and maintaining parks, plazas, and communal areas in the spirit of Nomashae's elemental values. Spaces where citizens gather, rest, and connect.
Tenders & Contracts
Issuing, reviewing, and awarding public construction tenders. All contracts are awarded transparently and in the best interest of the Republic.
Maintenance & Repair
Ensuring all existing infrastructure remains safe, functional, and up to standard. Regular inspections and repairs keep Nomashae running smoothly.
How We Build

Development Priorities

The order in which infrastructure projects are planned and executed

1
Capital Infrastructure Uni Citi and the government quarter are built first — establishing the seat of the Republic before expanding outward.
2
Regional Connectivity Roads and transit links connecting all seven regions — so no citizen is isolated from the rest of Nomashae.
3
Essential Utilities Power, water, and sanitation infrastructure in every inhabited region before non-essential projects proceed.
4
Public & Cultural Spaces Parks, plazas, and communal buildings that bring citizens together and reflect Nomashae's elemental identity.
5
Regional Development Projects Citizen-proposed works and regional improvements, reviewed and funded as resources allow.
How We Help

Citizen Services

What citizens can contact this ministry for

Apply for a Building Tender
Citizens may apply to take on public works contracts. All applications are reviewed by the Ministry and awarded based on capability and proposal quality.
Report Infrastructure Issues
Found a broken road, damaged structure, or failing public utility? Report it to the Ministry and it will be logged, prioritised, and addressed.
Propose a Public Works Project
Citizens can submit proposals for new infrastructure projects in their region. All proposals are reviewed and considered for the national development plan.
Project Status Enquiries
Want to know the status of an active or planned project in your region? Contact the Ministry for a full update on any ongoing works.
