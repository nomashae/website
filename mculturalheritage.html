What We Do

Responsibilities

The six pillars of cultural governance in Nomashae

Language Preservation
Protecting and promoting Flyingo Bisdomic — Nomashae's constructed national language — alongside Macedonian and English. Every citizen's linguistic identity is respected and celebrated.
Arts & Creative Expression
Supporting artists, animators, writers, musicians, and creators across all regions. The Ministry funds and promotes creative work that reflects Nomashae's identity and values.
Heritage & History
Documenting and preserving Nomashae's history from its founding on 1 September 2025. All national records, decrees, and cultural milestones are archived by this ministry.
National Anthem & Symbols
Stewardship of Nomashae's national symbols — the flag, the coat of arms, and the anthem. The Ministry ensures these are used with dignity and made accessible to all citizens.
Elemental Traditions
Honouring the Avatar: The Last Airbender-inspired traditions woven into Nomashae's identity — elemental philosophy, regional customs, and the values of balance and harmony.
Media & Public Broadcasting
Overseeing the Nomashae Media Group, including The Daily Scroll. The Ministry ensures citizens have access to accurate, culturally rich public media that reflects the Republic's voice.
Our Tongue

Languages of Nomashae

Nomashae is a multilingual Republic — every language spoken by its citizens is a part of its heritage

Flyingo Bisdomic — Official National Language

Flyingo Bisdomic is Nomashae's constructed language — created by its founders as a unique cultural identity for the Republic. A phonetically simplified language built on an English base, it features 10+ grammatical cases and a distinct sound system. It is the official language of all government documents, ceremonies, and public communications within the Republic.

Constructed Language 10+ Grammatical Cases Phonetic Simplification Official Government Language

Macedonian

Spoken by the founders and used in regional communications. Macedonian (Cyrillic script) is a recognised co-language of the Republic and features in all trilingual government materials alongside Flyingo Bisdomic and English.

Co-language Cyrillic Script

English

Used as Nomashae's international communication language and the base from which Flyingo Bisdomic was constructed. All public-facing government pages are available in English as standard.

Elemental Arts & Traditions

The cultural traditions of each of Nomashae's regions — rooted in the world of Avatar: The Last Airbender

Air · Freedom & Wisdom
Air Nomad Arts
The traditions of freedom, movement, and meditation. Air culture celebrates music carried on the breeze, nomadic storytelling, and the philosophy of non-attachment — finding peace in the journey rather than the destination.
Water · Adaptability & Flow
Water Tribe Arts
A culture of community, resilience, and flowing creativity. Water Tribe traditions include oral storytelling, communal crafts, and an artistic philosophy that adapts and finds new forms — like water itself.
Earth · Stability & Endurance
Earth Kingdom Arts
Rooted in the enduring traditions of the land — architecture, sculpture, and the written word. Earth Kingdom culture values permanence, legacy, and the kind of art that outlasts the artist.
Fire · Passion & Progress
Fire Nation Arts
Intense, passionate, and driven. Fire Nation traditions embrace theatre, performance, and the arts of ambition — creative works that burn bright and push boundaries. In Nomashae, Fire represents renewal and creative drive.
Balance · All Four Elements
Nomashalian Identity
Nomashae's unique cultural identity is the harmony of all four elements. No single tradition dominates — instead, the Republic celebrates the intersection of air, water, earth, and fire as a reflection of its founding values.
How We Help

Citizen Services

What citizens can contact this ministry for

Submit Creative Work
Citizens can submit art, music, writing, animations, or any creative work for inclusion in Nomashae's cultural archive or public platforms.
Access the National Archive
All official national documents, historical records, decrees, and cultural materials are publicly accessible through the Ministry of Culture & Heritage.
Flyingo Bisdomic Resources
Citizens seeking to learn or contribute to the development of Flyingo Bisdomic can access the language guide and contact the Ministry for support.
National Symbols & Usage
Questions about how to use Nomashae's flag, coat of arms, or anthem correctly? The Ministry provides official guidelines for all national symbols.
Media & Press Enquiries
For enquiries related to The Daily Scroll or the Nomashae Media Group, contact the Ministry of Culture & Heritage as the overseeing body.
